# REVEL Partner Brief - Client Portal v1

## Executive Summary

- REVEL is building a role-based wedding operating layer that makes planning and execution easier for everyone in the event ecosystem.
- Access is event-scoped and permissioned: clients, vendors, planners, and guests only see what they need for that specific wedding.
- The strategic moat is operational intelligence from real weddings, not generic software features.
- Our first commercialization wedge is two high-leverage tools:
  - Fusion Flow Experience Architect (client-facing premium lead magnet)
  - Venue Production Analyzer (planner and venue authority engine)
- The portal converts price-shopping conversations into outcome-driven consultations.
- The business objective is to increase package value, reduce coordination overhead, and improve referral velocity.

## Positioning Statement

REVEL is the AI operating system for wedding execution, designed to reduce chaos, speed decisions, and improve outcomes across couples, vendors, planners, and guests.

## Core Access Rule

Only participants in a specific wedding receive access to that wedding's portal data and tools.

- Client access: full planning and decision modules for their own event.
- Vendor access: scoped operational modules for assigned events.
- Planner access: timeline, dependencies, and coordination intelligence for assigned events.
- Guest access: concierge and logistics support for that wedding only.
- Post-event access: disabled by default unless intentionally extended.

## Why This Wins

1. It improves outcomes for every stakeholder, not just one user type.
2. It embeds REVEL into the full event workflow, not just entertainment moments.
3. It creates network effects: each wedding becomes a distribution surface for future weddings.
4. It compounds proprietary intelligence from execution patterns over time.

## High-Leverage Features (Current + Near-Term)

### 1) Fusion Flow Experience Architect

Purpose:
- Convert premium couples earlier by giving a customized production vision before consult.

Inputs:
- Cultural background blend, guest count, venue, event priorities, vibe preferences.

Outputs:
- AI-generated vibe deck with timeline, transitions, cultural moments, and lighting direction.

Business impact:
- Anchors consultation around value and outcomes instead of commodity DJ pricing.

### 2) Venue Production Analyzer

Purpose:
- Position REVEL as technical authority for venue-specific production feasibility.

Inputs:
- Venue name, room, guest count, target experience level.

Outputs:
- Constraints, production baseline, risk flags, and recommended technical package.

Business impact:
- Converts premium production from optional upsell to structural requirement.

### 3) AI Concierge (Portal Foundation)

Purpose:
- Provide always-on routing and planning support.

Outputs:
- Answers, next best actions, and directed workflow paths.

### 4) Coordination Workspace

Purpose:
- Reduce timeline churn and communication overhead.

Outputs:
- Role-based timeline updates, assignment visibility, and change tracking.

## Operating Principles

- Every workflow must save time or reduce rework.
- Recommendations must show assumptions and confidence.
- Sensitive instructions (family preferences, do-not-play constraints) require strict policy enforcement.
- Critical actions must be logged with owner and timestamp.

## 90-Day Rollout Plan

### Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4)

- Launch Fusion Flow and Venue Analyzer as lead magnets.
- Capture emails and route to consult path.
- Track event instrumentation for all CTA and module interactions.

### Phase 2 (Weeks 5-8)

- Enforce authentication and role-based access control.
- Add planner/vendor scoped views for active weddings.
- Add data freshness and trust metadata in outputs.

### Phase 3 (Weeks 9-12)

- Launch guest concierge pilot for selected weddings.
- Add day-of recovery prompts and timeline adjustment intelligence.
- Review KPI movement and lock v2 priorities.

## KPI Framework

- Consultation conversion rate from portal interactions.
- Average booked package value.
- Time to first qualified recommendation.
- Reduction in planning message back-and-forth.
- Reduction in day-of escalation incidents.
- Planner and vendor referral frequency.
